A Phred quality score (Q score) is the most common metric used to assess the accuracy of a sequence base from sequencing platforms.
It is an integer value representing the estimated probability of a sequencing error (P), i.e. that the base is incorrect.

Q=-10*log 10P

Q20 means the accuracy of the base is 99% (1% chance to be wrong), while Q30 means the base accuracy is ~ 99.9%.




Data Trimming Process
BaseCall Conversion
Illumina's official basecalling program, the bcl2fastq conversion software v2.20, was used to convert BCL files from all Illumina sequencing platforms into FASTQ reads.


Adaptor Clipping
Adaptors are short nucleotide sequences which have to be ligated to every single DNA molecule during library preparation, and allow DNA fragments to bind to a flow cell for next generation sequencing. Removal of adapter sequences, a process called 'adaptor trimming', or clipping, is one of the first steps in analyzing NGS data.


Sequence Quality Trimming
Quality trimming was proformed to remove low quality reads/bases. Lower quality bases from the the 3' end were removed using a sliding-window approach as the per base quality gradually drops toward the 3' end of reads.

Both adaptor clipping and sequence quality trimming were performed using Trimmomatic v0.36 with a sliding-window approach.
